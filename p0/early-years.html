The Birth of a Franchise

Founding of the Team

The New England Patriots were founded in 1960 as a charter member of the American Football League (AFL). The team originally played as the Boston Patriots, and their first home games were held at Boston Universityâ€™s Nickerson Field. The team struggled to find success in the early years, but they quickly became known for their passionate fan base.

1960s and Early 1970s

In their early years, the Patriots faced challenges both on and off the field. The team struggled to compete with more established franchises but gradually built a competitive roster. The arrival of head coach Chuck Fairbanks in 1973 helped elevate the team's performance and began to shape its identity.

Transition to Foxborough

In 1971, the team moved to Foxborough, Massachusetts, and began playing at the newly constructed Schaefer Stadium (later known as Foxborough Stadium). This move marked a new era for the franchise and laid the foundation for future success.

Key Moments

Throughout their early years, the Patriots experienced several key moments that shaped the franchise's history. Notably, they reached the playoffs for the first time in 1963, marking a significant milestone in their development. Despite early struggles, the foundation was being laid for a franchise that would become one of the most successful in NFL history.
